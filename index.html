Nopal con huevos


Description: Thin slices of nopal fried with eggs, tomatoes, onions, served on a tortilla and garnished with a cheese of your choice.
Nopal is an extremely common ingredient where we live in Central Mexico. But first, what is nopal?
Our first few weeks in Mexico, I felt like I kept seeing nopal listed as a primary ingredient in several dishes, but I could not figure out what it was. In my translator app, nopal translated directly to nopal. On an outing with our Spanish teacher and her husband, Hector, I was tempted to order a nopal smoothie, just to find out what it was! Hector merely laughed and said I wouldn't like it. I asked him what "nopal" meant, and he said "Nopal is nopal!"

At a restaurant, I saw a salad of steamed nopal with onions, which clued me in to it probably being a savory dish, and I started to suspect it was from a cactus by its vibrant green color and relatively flat shape. At the grocery store, I saw dried and puffed nopal snacks that looked like dry, green Cheetos, which didn't inspire a lot of confidence in them being true-to-life examples of what nopal really is. If I was going to try it, I was going to try it for real, not disguised into a bar food snack. Finally, I had my breakthrough at HEB when I saw a worker cleaning spines off what looked like prickly pear cactus nodes with a small machete. Directly in front of her were containers labeled "Nopales frescos," and more containers with the slightly more olive-toned cooked nopal salad-type mixture. Yes! Mystery solved!

There are many ways to use nopal, which I have heard compared in flavor and texture to okra. Not having had okra, I cannot confirm, but I will contend that it has a bright, citrusy flavor that makes for a wonderful brunch meal when paired with eggs, tomatoes, and onions and served on tortillas. That's what we will be making today!

A nopal dish similar to ours.

Ingredients

Serves 2 hungry people.

  • 4 eggs, beaten
  • oil for frying
  • 1 node of nopal, sliced
  • cherry tomatoes, halved or chopped
  • half of a medium onion, chopped
  • salt to taste
  • 4 tortillas, small size (corn or flour depending on your preference)
  • cilantro for garnish
  • cheese for garnish (try cotija or cheddar!)
  • your favorite salsa or hot sauce (optional)

Steps

  1. Prepare a medium sauté pan with a tablespoon or more of oil over medium high heat.
  2. Sauté the nopal and onions until the onions are translucent. Add the tomatoes and stir until heated through.
  3. Reduce heat to medium. Pour the egg mixture over the sauteed vegetables and scramble together. Add salt toward the end of cooking.
  4. At the same time, prepare your tortillas. If using corn, quickly fry in a small pan. If flour, heat as desired.
  5. Divide the scrambled mixture over the tortillas, garnishing with cilantro and cheese.
  6. Serve with salsa or hot sauce.
